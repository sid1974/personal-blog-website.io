sapta sagaradaache ello - Side A.

By:LAZY GANG
sep-11, 2023 - 12:20

Priya (Rukmini Vasanth), a middle-class girl, wants simple things in life with her partner, Manu (Rakshit Shetty). Together, they see a dream. However, Manu’s dream is mostly to ensure that he gives Priya the life she deserves and dreams of. In a bid to achieve that, he crosses a line and lands in jail. Amid the chaos around, Priya and Manu find solace in each other. They are, undoubtedly, each other’s’ home. Separated by prison bars, they draw energy from each other. But, can they do it forever? SSE is a slow-burner. The film takes off slowly, establishing the characters and building a world around them. The music, cinematography and the colour palette keeps one glued to the screen. The second half hits like a breeze on a hot summer day. The film’s strength lies in its performances. Rakshit’s performance is layered around the seven stages of grief. Rukmini’s smile is infectious and haunting. Her simple wardrobe, in several shades of blue, enhances the charm of this beach-loving character. The dialogues bordering on philosophy, is inundated with metaphors. Senior artistes Achyut Kumar, Sharath Lohitashwa, Ramesh Indira, Gopalakrishna Deshapande and Pavitra Lokesh help the protagonists build a real, vulnerable and believable world around them. If Rakshit and Rukmini are the on-screen heroes, director Hemanth, music composer Charan Raj and cinematographer Advaitha Gurumurthy are the off-screen heroes. Hemanth’s last two films, Godhi Banna, Sadharana Maikattu and Kavaludaari, were savoury packages. Though SSE revolves around the world of crime, the director has not succumbed to the pressure of including unnecessary commercial elements.
